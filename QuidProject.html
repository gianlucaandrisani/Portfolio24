Quid

Problem

Diabetes type one leads to many stressful and unpleasant situations in social and daily life bringing discomfort even for the easiest things.
How to design a system which allows Diabetes' patients to be more carefree and normalize some gestures they need to do due to the condition management and medication processes?

Solution

Quid is a medical system designed in order to give much more carefree to type 1 diabetes patients, comforting them with the total control and a simple and easy system to manage.

Outcome

The final system solution that we showed to the users made them incredibly happy and interested, even contacting us the following day recalling the experience and how much they'd like it to be their daily driver!

User Flows

In order to understand which were the most important things for a diabetes patient, we built the user flows to reach the most important actions and information immediately, effortless and safely. This user flows where built with the aid of the users, asking them what were they expecting from the app and the interaction from it.









This user flow maps all the possibilities that the user has once opened the application, having everything under control he will be able to check his glycemic status at first, and also to check the condition of his product. With this method, the user has the most important information available at first sight.







This user flow instead explores the other most important feature of our product, within thw food section, the user will be able to inject the right amount of insulin, and will also get aid from the system itself.

Solution iteration

Starting from the information architecture, I developed the wireframed app, without never forgetting the medical aim of the project, how should the user feel and what he is expecting to find.

Particular emphasis has been put on the injection flow, being the most delicate, I studied it in depth. Starting from the gestural constraints, it was needed to make the injection selection and action natural and normal, rather than the current state of the art which forces users to enact weird and old injection movements using the insulin pen. The system has been designed in order to make users interact only with the app when in social gatherings, managing anything from there and having full control about their condition and related actions.

Insulin Injection

Effortless and normalized injection process

The current state-of-the-art products, force users to enact uncomfortable and socially awkward gestures to do their injection process, with the following flow, users will be free to focus on their life while the system will manage the rest.

The User Interface has been designed with the aim of creative a calm and controlled environment which could still express safety and cleaning, here's explained why the contrast between blue and green colors.
The dimensions of the typeface are due to the eye problems that Diabetics people might face because of their condition, being the eyes' cornea one of the first body area to be reached to collect and compensate the missing sugar in the blood, possibly leading to sight deficits, here's also the reason for such strong contrasts.

Also, most of the screen are the designed to show fewer information and elements, to still make users feel in total control over their condition focusing on few important elements at a time that could have big impact on users' condition, e.g. food quantity screen…

The injection itself, can be slightly painful, causing a burning and unpleasant sensation, providing the gimmick of motions and animations, we'll focus user's attention on something else rather than the injection itself, alleviating, as possible, the hurtful circumstances that users face.

Where does all of it come from?

What is Diabetes?

Diabetes is a chronic disease characterized by the presence of high levels blood glucose (hyperglycemia) and due to an altered quantity or function of insulin. Insulin is the hormone produced by the pancreas that allows glucose to enter cells and its consequent use as an energy source. When this mechanism is altered, glucose accumulates in the bloodstream

Diabetes Type 1

It affects about 10% of people with diabetes and generally occurs in childhood or adolescence. In type 1 diabetes, the pancreas does not produce insulin due to the destruction of the ß cells that produce this hormone: it therefore needs to be injected every day and for life.

Customer Journey Insights

Human Body extension

Our studies allowed us to design and test this organic form that become an extension of the human body.

Quid offers a totally flexible system, which adapts to the user’s needs and behaviors, decomposable in different rechargeable parts, so the user can reuse them and not be forced to trash the whole product with the technology inside.





User testing

We involved diabetic patients in our design process for the most harsh and important choices we made, it was a very delicate project, and we wanted to design things with sense and that could actually change some people’s lives.

Engineering collaboration

Also, the system has been designed alongside with a Biomedical Engineer, who was helping us to shape the most technically feasible product, considering all the possible chemical and physical constraints, always reporting what could have been done, improved or also when to take some steps back.

Key Takeaways

The project need an in-depth development from the gaming point of view, proving a broad range of options to offer to keep children engaged and interested in paying with it over the time. Also, the development of the app and its content over the time will be required, to always satisfy kids interests and new games.
