So what is it?

Phrion would allow users to have delightful retail experiences within the comfort of their own homes. The only thing they'll need to do is to scan their surroundings. The software will then overlap a digital layer to the recorded physical one, making them correspond perfectly, to let users walk and interact with elements as they were there and in a physical form.







Move around as in real life

The Phrion system is studied in order to let users perform normal movements and gestures, moving around freely and interacting with digital elements.

VR Prototype

C# and Unity Prototype.

The successful production of the prototype was a crucial and delicate moment of our projects, very challenging and alienating as well. It was fundamental to test the idea with users and professionals, to validate the idea and bring it to even more people.

3D Scanning

The University provided us with a room where we could manipulate and move objects around as we preferred, to then scan it and build our experience starting from that specific space.

we used a LIDAR 3D scanner to precisely scan the space and easily export it into the .obj format. the 3D file has been then imported into Unity, to build the actual experience. adding then interaction features to the experience coding in C# and applying code to specific objects.

Resulting in a digital environment that will respect users' physical constraints, where digital elements are positioned only where physical ones are already there.

Interactable elements

In the digital environment, several interactable elements have been added, with which users can easily interact or learn and read information that they need for the occasion. This would be a brand-new way of how users interact with technology, businesses and products.

The store in these frames is designed following Vertiigine's brand guidelines and style, respecting its values and aesthetics.
